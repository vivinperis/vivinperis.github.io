Agentic Connector Harness
Rippling·2024 - Now·50+ engineers
Problem
Every managed connector at Rippling re-invented its eval setup, error handling, and AI glue. Building a new connector took engineering teams 2-3 months, and quality varied wildly.
Approach
A unified harness: CDK-style platform, declarative skill files per connector, built-in LLM judges, a traffic simulator for replaying production calls, and a fully automated eval pipeline.
Outcome
50+ engineers across orgs ship on it; new-connector build time is days, not months; eval coverage held above 80% in production; harness became the foundation for connecting customer data to Rippling AI itself.

04

How I Build

Three principles that keep showing up in the work I'm proud of.

- 01
Leverage compounds.
A platform 50 engineers ship on beats a feature 5 use. Most of my highest-impact work - the connector harness, the aggregation engine, Ula's founding services - was infrastructure other people built on. Build for the next person, not the next demo.
- 02
For AI, evals are the spec.
A prompt without an eval is a guess. The harness ships before the demo. Judges, simulators, and regression sets are the thing that turns an LLM call into a feature you can stake a customer on.
- 03
Boring infra, exciting products.
Pick the proven tools so the surprises happen in customer impact - not at 3am on-call. Kafka, Druid, JDBC, Flink, Postgres. The job is shipping, not collecting new toys.
